Victoria University

The parliament passed an act in 1897 that established Victoria University of Wellington. It was the 60th anniversary of Queen Victoria's coronation in 1897, it was given the name Victoria.

Find out about Victoria University

Victoria University of Wellington's identity embodies exactly who they are: a globally-minded university that prioritises close involvement in the social, cultural, and economic life of its city and region, or, to put it another way, a global–civic university with their marae at the centre of it all.

Building an international reputation requires a distinct character, and the cornerstone of identity is place—in their instance, the city of Wellington in Aotearoa New Zealand. As a result, and to highlight the University's long-standing relationship with the capital city, their brand emphasises and promotes the name Wellington.
